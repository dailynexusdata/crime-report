IVFP Crime Data 2013, 2018, 2019

Alternative policing policies focused on addressing nonviolent crime were presented by the Isla Vista Foot Patrol to the Isla Vista Community Services District in the April 27 Board of Directors meeting, after evaluating policing trends in Isla Vista from previous years.

The Isla Vista Foot Patrol made crime data from 2013, 2018 and 2019 available which shows that the majority of crimes committed in Isla Vista are nonviolent. This data also reveals that though only making up around 3% of the population, Black people made up around 6% of crimes. These two statistics in particular lead to the consideration of new policing programs.

SNAP (Student Neighborhood Assistance Program) in San Luis Obispo was presented as an example program for non-violent response focused on educating residents about noise ordinance laws as a first response before having police issue tickets for later offences.

Current nonviolent responses in Isla Vista include the Safety Stations on Friday and Saturday nights, Santa Barbara County’s CARES program for mental health emergencies and the Isla Vista Foot Patrol Party Registration.

From the data made available, the majority of crimes can be classified as related to alcohol or partying. The alcohol group includes charges such as minor in possession, open container and being drunk in public. The partying group includes loud noise/music and festival ordinance violations.

Of the top 10 crime categories, only 5.5% of the crime was violent. These violent crimes include resisting arrest, fighting which includes battery and fighting in public, and robberies, burglaries and theft.

Crimes resulted in either Arrests, Citations or no charges were filed. Of all interactions with police officers resulting in a charge in 2013, 2018 and 2019, black people had the highest percentage resulting in arrests. The majority of crimes resulted in citations (72%) where 12% did not result in filing a charge.

Alcohol related offenses make up the majority of crimes and this is consistent across all age groups. Ages below 25 are responsible for most of the partying and disorderly conduct charges. 21 year olds commit more crimes than other age groups with 2/3rds of the crime being committed by 18-23 year olds.
